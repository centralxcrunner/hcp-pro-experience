HCP AI — Customer Context Service

Engineering Requirement · Chris Kosednar → Montrell Williams · 2026-03-17
A shared backend service that enriches any HCP AI interaction with full customer history the moment a caller or texter is identified. Build it once — every channel consumes it.
📞 Voice (HCP AI) — inbound calls today
💬 SMS (HCP AI) — inbound texts, coming soon
⚡ Future channels — any interaction needing customer history
Two resolution mechanisms: (1) Automatic lookup at interaction start using phone number — context is ready before the AI says a word. (2) A get_customer_context(customer_id) tool call the AI can invoke mid-conversation once identity is established through dialogue. Together these handle the happy path and the edge case — a household member on a different number, a texter who hasn't interacted before.

🔧 Mechanism 2 — Tool Call: get_customer_context(customer_id)

When the phone number lookup returns no match, the AI starts the call without customer context. But during the conversation, the caller may reveal they're an existing customer — e.g., a spouse calling from their own phone who references a job, an appointment, or gives an address on file. When the AI determines a customer_id through conversation, it calls this tool to retrieve and inject full context at that point.

🔵 When to call this tool

AI has identified the customer through conversation

Caller provides name + address that matches a record, references a specific job or estimate number, or confirms details that uniquely identify them. Customer_id is known with reasonable confidence before the tool is called.

🟣 When NOT to call this tool

Speculative or low-confidence identity

Do not call based on name alone (common names), or if the caller hasn't confirmed enough details to establish identity. False positives risk injecting the wrong customer's data into the call.

Open question for eng: How does the AI obtain a customer_id through conversation? Options: (a) AI asks for name + address, backend resolves to customer_id and returns it for the tool call, (b) secondary lookup happens transparently and AI is handed a customer_id to confirm. Engineering to propose the resolution flow.

❓ Open Questions for Engineering
  • 1What's the realistic latency for phone number lookup + context assembly before the AI's opening line? What fields are fast vs. require joins?
  • 2For get_customer_context: how does the AI obtain a valid customer_id through conversation? Does it ask name + address, then the backend resolves to customer_id, then AI calls the tool? Or does a secondary lookup happen transparently first?
  • 3What's our false-positive risk on identity resolution? If a caller says "it's Sarah" and there are 3 customers named Sarah at this pro — how do we disambiguate before the tool call fires?
  • 4What's the context window budget for injected data? What's the priority order if we hit the limit — what gets truncated first?
  • 5Are prior call transcripts stored in summarized form already, or only raw audio/text? What's the pipeline from Deepgram/Vapi → structured summary we can inject?
  • 6Which financial fields (invoice totals, estimate amounts, billing) should never be surfaced as a raw value to the AI? Engineering to define what's read-only or flag-only.
  • 7Multi-record households: phone number matches multiple customers at the same pro (e.g., family business). How do we handle?

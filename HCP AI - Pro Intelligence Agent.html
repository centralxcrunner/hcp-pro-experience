HCP AI — Pro Intelligence Agent

Engineering Requirement · Chris Kosednar → Montrell Williams · 2026-03-17
A daily batch agent that reads a Pro's last 180 days of completed jobs — titles, descriptions, line items, and technician notes — and distills them into a structured intelligence profile. This is the richest possible signal for what a Pro actually does. Settings tell you what they configured. Job history tells you what they've built a business doing.
🕐 Runs: Once per day per active Pro
📥 Input: 180 days of completed jobs
📤 Output: Pro Intelligence Profile written to context store
🔌 Consumed by: Pro Context Service at session start
🧠 Why Job History Beats Settings

Settings + Pricebook

  • Configured once and forgotten — often out of date
  • Lists services a Pro added to the catalog, not what they actually get called for
  • Prices often not filled in or set to generic defaults
  • Tells you nothing about how jobs actually go — what gets noted, what goes wrong
  • No signal on what the Pro is good at vs. what's a stretch job
  • No seasonal or geographic pattern — uniform flat list

180 Days of Job History

  • Reflects what the Pro actually does — frequency is truth
  • Real job descriptions written by techs in the field — natural language, specific
  • Actual completed invoice amounts — what customers really pay
  • Tech notes reveal common issues, access instructions, customer context
  • Job volume and type by month — reveals seasonality and core vs. edge work
  • Geographic coverage from actual job addresses — not configured radius
The core insight: A Pro who's done 200 HVAC tune-ups in the last 6 months is an HVAC business, even if their pricebook has 40 other service categories. The AI should talk like it knows that. Right now it doesn't.

Separation of concerns: This agent runs offline, once per day. It is not in the call path — the profile it generates is pre-written to a store and fetched at session start like any other Pro context field. Zero latency impact on live interactions.
📋 What the Agent Produces — Intelligence Profile Fields

The agent reads raw job data and writes a structured profile. Fields marked AGENT are LLM-generated summaries. Fields marked P1 are direct aggregations from job records.

Field Description / What It Enables Type
📊 Service Patterns — What They Actually Do
top_services[]
→ name, job_count, pct_of_volume		 Top service types ranked by job count over 180 days. The AI's primary signal for what this Pro does. "You've called the right place — AC repair is our most common job." AI should lead with confidence on high-volume services. P1
services_not_in_top_10[] Services that appear rarely (<2% of volume) — these are edge jobs. AI should express appropriate uncertainty or escalate to a human rather than over-committing on low-volume work. P1
typical_job_description_samples[] Verbatim job title + description samples from the last 90 days, one per top service type. Gives the AI the Pro's actual language for their work — how their technicians describe jobs. Informs natural, specific conversation. P1
seasonal_pattern LLM summary of volume and service mix by month. "This Pro sees 3x HVAC volume June–August, slower in Jan–Feb." Helps AI set accurate expectations on availability and lead time. AGENT
emergency_job_rate % of jobs flagged as emergency or same-day. High rate = Pro is comfortable with urgency; low rate = they likely don't prioritize emergency calls. AI should calibrate its urgency language accordingly. P1
📍 Geographic Coverage — Where They Actually Work
active_zip_codes[]
→ zip, job_count		 Zip codes where jobs were actually completed, ranked by volume. Ground truth service area — more reliable than configured radius. AI can confidently confirm coverage for high-volume zips. P1
active_cities[] Cities derived from job addresses, ranked by frequency. AI can say "we work all over [City A] and [City B]" based on real data. P1
farthest_job_distance_miles Maximum distance from business address where a job was completed — real upper bound on how far they've traveled. P1
💰 Pricing — What Customers Actually Pay
price_ranges[]
→ service_type, p25, median, p75		 Actual invoice amounts from completed jobs, by service type. 25th/50th/75th percentile. Far more accurate than pricebook entries. Engineering to determine whether AI may quote ranges or only acknowledge "we'll provide a firm quote on-site." P1
avg_job_value, median_job_value Overall job economics — AI uses this to calibrate whether a caller's ask is typical or a larger-than-usual engagement. P1
common_add_ons[] Line items that frequently appear alongside primary services. Signals natural upsell opportunities. "When we do AC tune-ups we usually check refrigerant levels — want me to include that?" P1
📝 Technician Notes — The Richest Signal
common_note_themes[] LLM-extracted recurring themes from tech notes across 180 days — e.g., "gate code required at most commercial jobs", "customers frequently ask about warranty on parts", "dogs present at residential calls". These are patterns the AI couldn't know from settings. AGENT
common_access_instructions LLM summary of recurring access notes — gate codes, parking, building entry. Helps AI set correct expectations with new customers ("our tech will call ahead for any access details"). AGENT
frequent_customer_concerns[] Recurring topics noted by techs after jobs — pricing questions, warranty questions, follow-up requests. Trains the AI on what this Pro's customers actually care about. AGENT
quality_signals LLM summary of positive indicators from job notes and outcomes — repeat customers, referrals mentioned, 5-star reviews noted inline. The AI can draw on these as credibility signals without making things up. AGENT
👷 Team Performance
technicians_by_volume[]
→ name, job_count, top_service_type		 Which technicians are doing the most work and in which service categories. "Can I get the same tech who came last time?" — AI can speak to technician specializations with confidence. P1
avg_jobs_per_day Typical daily job volume — gives the AI a realistic sense of how busy this Pro is when discussing availability. P1
📣 Pro Narrative — Ready to Inject
pro_summary_paragraph A 3–4 sentence LLM-generated summary of this Pro's business, written in the AI's voice — based entirely on job history. Injected directly into the system prompt. Example: "This is a high-volume HVAC company primarily serving the Denver metro area. Their most common jobs are AC tune-ups, furnace repairs, and refrigerant recharges. They run 8–12 jobs per day across a team of 4 technicians, with a strong track record of same-day availability for emergencies." AGENT
On LLM-generated fields: The agent uses an LLM to distill patterns from raw job text — not to answer caller questions. The output is structured data written to the context store once per day, reviewed by quality checks before injection. The LLM in the call path only reads the pre-generated profile — it never calls back to raw job data in real time.

❓ Open Questions for Engineering
  • 1How many active Pros have 180 days of job history? What's the distribution of job volume — are there Pros with 5 jobs and Pros with 2,000 in that window? Agent behavior needs to gracefully handle both extremes.
  • 2What's the right LLM for the distillation step — cost vs. quality tradeoff at scale across all Pros daily. Engineering to size the per-Pro token budget and total daily cost.
  • 3Tech notes are free-text and sometimes contain PII (customer names, addresses, phone numbers written inline). Agent must strip or mask PII before injecting into the AI-readable profile.
  • 4How do we handle Pros who've had HCP AI enabled for less than 180 days? Shorter windows are fine — agent should note the data range so the AI doesn't over-claim ("based on your last 60 days of jobs...").
  • 5Quality gate: what happens if the agent produces a clearly bad profile — e.g., a Pro with 3 jobs, all in a different trade than their current focus? Flag for review rather than injecting bad signal.
  • 6Where does the profile live in the context store and how does the Pro Context Service fetch it? Define the interface between this agent's output and the session-start injection pipeline.
